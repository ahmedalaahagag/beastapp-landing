About Suvo

Suvo is a supplement tracking app for people who want a clearer view of what they take, when they take it, and where conflicts may exist inside a supplement stack.

Who maintains Suvo

Suvo is maintained by the Suvo team. The app and website focus on product education, supplement cataloging, timing reminders, and source-backed interaction context.

How to contact us

For support, privacy requests, source corrections, product-pick questions, or partnership inquiries, email hello@getsuvo.com.

How source rules are reviewed

Suvo separates sources by strength. Regulatory authorities and national reference bodies are used first for safety thresholds. Government fact sheets, systematic reviews, drug labels, and curated clinical databases add interaction and timing context. Chemical registries are used for compound identity and synonyms, not clinical guidance.

When sources disagree, Suvo keeps user-facing guidance conservative and avoids presenting uncertain findings as personalized instructions. Public source notes are available in the source library.

Not medical advice

Suvo provides general educational information. It does not diagnose, treat, prevent, or clear any condition, medication, or supplement regimen. Users should consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting or changing supplements, especially when taking medication, preparing for surgery, pregnant or breastfeeding, or managing a diagnosed condition.
